Professional Development Journal

Settling In

During my first two weeks at UCS studying Computing and Digital Technologies, I focused on understanding the structure of the course and expectations at university level. I familiarised myself with the learning platforms, assessment methods, and module requirements. I began using Visual Studio Code and refreshed my knowledge of basic programming concepts such as variables, control structures, and problem-solving logic. I also worked on organising my workflow, setting up folders correctly and practising clean file management. Evidence of my progress includes early programming exercises and structured notes from lectures. To improve, I plan to strengthen my time management skills and become more confident asking questions in class. I aim to build stronger foundations in problem-solving so I can approach future assignments with greater efficiency and independence.

Building Foundations

In weeks three and four, I began applying programming fundamentals in practical tasks. I developed small programs using functions, loops, and conditional statements. I improved my understanding of debugging and testing by identifying and correcting logical errors in my code. I also started working more independently, researching solutions and experimenting with different approaches before asking for help. My evidence includes completed lab tasks and a small BMI calculator program demonstrating user input handling and structured functions. What went well was my ability to break problems into smaller steps and write clearer, more readable code. To improve, I need to deepen my understanding of how programs execute behind the scenes and practise writing more efficient solutions.

Working Professionally

During weeks five and six, I began focusing more on writing professional and maintainable code. I practised organising my projects properly, using meaningful variable names, and commenting my work clearly. I also gained more confidence using development tools such as VS Code and GitHub for version control. I learned the importance of planning before coding, especially when developing structured solutions. Evidence of this progress includes structured assignments with improved readability and better logical organisation. I feel my problem-solving skills have improved significantly, and I am becoming more confident approaching complex tasks. Moving forward, I want to develop stronger analytical thinking skills and begin exploring more advanced topics such as object-oriented programming and software design principles.
